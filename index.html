None. We do not operate any server or account system, and the Apps contain no

2. Where your data lives

  • On your device. Everything you enter — transactions, budgets, health readings, habits, pool and kitchen data — is stored locally on your device.
  • Your iCloud (Finora Budget and Vitals Flow, optional). Both apps can synchronize your data across your Apple devices using Apple's CloudKit private database. This uses your iCloud account and is managed entirely by Apple; we have no access to it. See Apple's privacy policy at https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/.
    • Sync is a Pro feature in both apps, and each has a Settings → iCloud Sync switch to turn it off. In Finora Budget it is on by default once Pro is active; in Vitals Flow you turn it on yourself. Either way it only ever uses your own iCloud — never a server of ours.
    • Smart Utility Hub does not use iCloud at all; its data stays on your device.
  • Apple Health (Vitals Flow only, optional). With your permission, Vitals Flow reads from and writes to HealthKit. That data remains in your Health store under your control, is never transmitted to us, and is never used for advertising or shared with third parties.

3. Optional AI features (bring-your-own-key)

Some features can use an AI provider to help you — for example scanning a receipt, reading
a blood-pressure monitor, analyzing a pool test strip, or giving pool guidance. **These
features are off by default and work only if you add your own** API key for a
provider you choose:

  • OpenAI — https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
  • Anthropic — https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy

When you use such a feature, the relevant content (for example the image or text you are
processing) is sent **directly from your device to the provider you configured, using your
own account and key.** We do not receive, store, or have access to that content, or to your
key. Your use of a provider is governed by that provider's terms and privacy policy.
To disable this entirely, simply do not add an API key (or remove it in Settings).

4. Device permissions

The Apps request only what a feature needs, and the data stays on your device unless a
section above says otherwise:

  • Camera — to scan receipts, a blood-pressure monitor, or a pool test strip.
  • Bluetooth — to connect to a compatible body or kitchen scale.
  • Face ID / Touch ID — to lock the app. Biometric authentication is handled by Apple's Secure Enclave; we never receive your biometric data.
  • Notifications — for reminders you set (e.g. habit or bill reminders).

5. Sharing and selling

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with anyone. We do not track
you across other apps or websites, so no App Tracking Transparency prompt is shown.

6. Data retention and deletion

Because your data is stored on your device (and optionally in your iCloud), you control
it. Deleting an App removes its on-device data. iCloud data is managed in your Apple ID /
iCloud settings. Any backups you export are files you save and control.

7. Children's privacy

The Apps are not directed to children under 13 and do not knowingly collect information from
children. Since we collect no data at all, no such information is gathered.

8. Changes to this policy
